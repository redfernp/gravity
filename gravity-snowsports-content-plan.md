### Section Descriptions

**Home:** High-impact visual entry point. Hero rotates between competition highlights, gear drops, and destination features. Tone is aggressive and design-forward. No fluff, no beginner content.

**Compete:** The editorial heartbeat. Covers X Games, Freeride World Tour, Dew Tour, and BRITS. Crucially, it owns the UK underground -- dry slope comps, university racing, grass-roots slopestyle events that nobody else covers at this level. This is the section that sets Gravity apart from every other UK snow media outlet.

**Shred:** Discipline-by-discipline editorial. Not instructional -- opinionated, experience-led, rider-voiced. Splitboarding and ski touring get serious treatment here, not a token mention.

**Gear Lab:** Primary monetisation engine. Every review is independent, opinionated, and written from real use. Featured brands (Dope Snow, Montec, and the full Brand Spotlights roster) get priority placement but reviews are honest. Trust is the asset.

**Destinations:** Resort and backcountry destination features. Slant toward less-covered, more extreme terrain (La Grave, Lyngen Alps, Hokkaido powder belts, Alaskan lines). UK indoor centres covered for off-season relevance.

**Culture:** Long-form interviews, film features, photographer spotlights, and opinion pieces. Builds brand depth beyond gear and comps.

**Brand Spotlights:** Dedicated editorial pages for each brand. Not ads -- deep-dive features that introduce the brand's DNA, feature their athletes, and link through to product reviews and gear guides across the site.

# We Are Gravity

Snow isn't a hobby for us. It's the thing everything else gets planned around.

Gravity Snowsports was built by three people who were tired of reading the same safe, glossy, beginner-friendly snow content and wondering where their version of the sport was. We wanted somewhere that talked about the Freeride World Tour like it mattered. Somewhere that actually covered what's happening on UK dry slopes and university circuits. Somewhere that reviewed gear with real opinions, not press-release recycling.

So we built it.

---

### The Team

**Jake Renshaw -- Freerider, Gear Obsessive, Northerner**

Jake grew up in Leeds, spent his teenage years lapping the dry slope at Yeadon, and had what he calls his "brain rewire moment" the first time he watched Candide Thovex's One Of Those Days. He has since spent every penny of disposable income getting to Chamonix, La Grave, and one deeply questionable trip to a side valley in Georgia that ended with a helicopter evacuation and absolutely zero regrets.

Jake runs Gear Lab. He tests everything. He bought three different hard shells in one season just to compare them properly. His family think he has a problem. He thinks he is thorough.

Skis wide, turns hard, does not own a single piece of green or blue kit.

**Cara McAllister -- Splitboarder, Ski Tourer, Reluctant Social Media Person**

Cara is from Inverness and has been riding Scottish lines since she was seventeen. She started splitboarding during a season working in Aviemore and never looked back. She now splits her time between the Cairngorms and the Lyngen Alps, where she spends two weeks every March skinning up things that make her friends back home go quiet on WhatsApp.

Cara covers backcountry, touring, and splitboard content on Gravity. She also writes our destination guides for anywhere that involves earning your turns. She knows more about snow science, avalanche terrain, and skin wax than anyone else we have ever met.

She agreed to run social media for the site on the condition that she never has to post a reel doing a trending sound. We respect that.

**Dom Ferreira -- Park Rider, Competition Nerd, Slopestyle Archivist**

Dom is from Bristol and has been riding park since he was twelve, starting at Chill Factore on his mum's membership. He competed in university snow sports for three years, never quite made the podium at BUSC, and decided the better use of his obsession was writing about the people who did.

Dom is our competition editor. He watches every X Games live stream from start to finish, has a spreadsheet of every FWT qualifier result since 2018, and has opinions about halfpipe judging criteria that he will share at length if you give him the opportunity.

He also covers the UK underground scene -- the dry slope comps, the grassroots slopestyle events, the riders who are two seasons away from being on a proper team. This is his favourite part of the job.

---

### Why Gravity

The UK has a genuinely brilliant snow sports community. University clubs, dry slope regulars, people who take two weeks off work every January and chase powder across three countries. Athletes coming up through BUSC who go on to compete at World Cup level. A backcountry scene in Scotland that would surprise you.

None of this gets covered properly. Most UK snow media is either resort marketing dressed up as journalism or gear content that reads like a catalogue.

We are not that.

We cover the sport the way we experience it -- obsessively, opinionatedly, with proper respect for the people actually doing it. We partner with brands we genuinely rate. We review gear honestly. We cover competitions from the grassroots up.

If that sounds like your kind of snow media, you are in the right place.

### Brand Feature Format

Each brand gets a dedicated feature article (1,500-2,000 words) structured as:

1. **Origin story** -- where the brand came from and why it matters
2. **Ethos and positioning** -- what they stand for, who they make kit for
3. **Athlete roster** -- who they back and at what level (X Games, FWT, grassroots)
4. **Hero products** -- 3-4 products reviewed or highlighted with affiliate links
5. **Who it's for** -- honest steer on what rider type gets the most from this brand
6. **Verdict** -- one-paragraph recommendation

### 301 Redirect Strategy

If the original domain had indexed pages, 301 redirect any crawlable legacy URLs to the most relevant new section:

| Legacy URL pattern | Redirect destination |
|---|---|
| /results/ or /race/ | /compete/uk-underground-scene/ |
| /members/ or /club/ | /about-us/ |
| /news/ | /culture/ |
| /links/ or /resources/ | /gear-lab/gear-guides/ |
| Any ungrouped URLs | / (homepage) |

Since the domain appears to have lapsed and likely has no indexed pages, a clean architecture launch is fine. Monitor Google Search Console from day one.

---

### Topical Authority -- Early Priorities

Google needs to understand what Gravity is within the first 60 days of indexing. Signal topical authority fast by:

1. **Publish the Compete section first** -- the BUSC and varsity race content directly mirrors the backlink anchor text from skiresults.co.uk and varsityrace.co.uk. This tells Google those links are contextually correct.

2. **Internal linking from day one** -- every gear review links to a relevant brand feature. Every competition piece links to a relevant rider profile. Every destination piece links to relevant gear.

3. **Build the Gear Lab pillar pages before individual reviews** -- the "Best Ski Jackets of 2026/27" pillar should exist before the individual jacket reviews go live. Pillar pages establish category authority and internal link targets.

4. **Backcountry safety content early** -- beacon guides, avalanche safety explainers, and touring kit guides attract links naturally from outdoor and mountain safety communities. High editorial trust signals.

5. **Avoid thin content at launch** -- do not publish placeholder pages. Every URL that goes live should be 800 words minimum. Gear reviews should be 1,200+ words. Pillar pieces 2,500+.
